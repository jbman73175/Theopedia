## Summary

Chapter 2 starts with “therefore”, showing that it is a
continuation of the main argument begun in Rom 1:18-32, in which
Paul explains the sinfulness of all humanity. However the language
moves from 3rd person (“they”) to 2nd person (“you”). This change,
first used in Rom 2:1, is explained in Rom 2:17. The “you” is
addressing Jewish members of the audience. This chapter addresses
the particularly Jewish response to Rom 1:18-32, and argues that
possession of the Jewish Law does not make a person any less guilty
of sin. This is because Jews, while having the law, still fail to
obey it completely.

Paul’s final comment in Rom 2:28-29 is a reference to Jewish
expectations of the coming age. Three of the expectations were that
God would circumcise his people’s hearts, would write the law on
their hearts, and would give the Spirit (see further discussion in
the [main page](Romans "Romans")).
